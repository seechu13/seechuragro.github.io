Why Lakadong Turmeric?

What is Lakadong?

Lakadong turmeric from Meghalaya’s Jaintia Hills is renowned for consistently high curcumin levels. Official state materials and recent press notes cite a typical range of 7–12% curcumin, far above common varieties (≈2–3%). :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

Mission Lakadong (Meghalaya)

Meghalaya launched Mission Lakadong to scale production, productivity and value addition, including FPO formation, testing, and processing infrastructure. Documented targets included increasing area from ~2,577 ha towards ~6,070 ha and lifting production and productivity substantially (e.g., production to ~50,000 MT; productivity to ~28 MT/ha). :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

In March–April 2024, Lakadong Turmeric was granted a Geographical Indication (GI) tag, strengthening origin-linked branding and authenticity enforcement for the Jaintia Hills region. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}

Adaptability to protected cultivation

While Lakadong is traditionally open-field grown, our plan is to adapt nursery establishment and early growth under protected / controlled environments to enhance disease management, uniformity and traceability; field growing and curing then follow best-practice protocols. This approach leverages protected-cultivation benefits (sanitation, climate buffering, input control) to meet residue and quality specs for premium buyers.
